Current prediction markets only differ from the Equities Market in these areas:
1. Order of magnitudes more liquidity and capital exists in the equities market.
2. There is no pre-determined (or identifiable) end time. In prediction markets, this is when the event 'ends'
- The caveat here would be US style options, but we will abstain from derivative markets for now.

This indicates that traditional trading strategies should be effective in prediction markets; i.e. 
simple moving averages, chart analysis, at face value.

Identifiable features:
1. Market becomes overbought or oversold (whether at the start, or during high volatility periods)
2. Buy and hold strategy (yes on the favorite side), akin to investing in ETFs, is the strategy to beat! Currently, this is a profitable strategy on prediction markets (albeit with a stop-loss and profit taking limit)
3. There always exists a mean, and mean reversion opportunities throughout the lifetime of a sports prediction market. Teams revert if they are hot, and go hot if they start cold.
4. ESPN's statscore is faster than Kalshi's statscore --> This is a guaranteed method but not likely. This was a visual observation throughout a single game.
